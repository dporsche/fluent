The Model

Six levels. One clear journey.

Everyone is somewhere on a spectrum from "knows AI matters but hasn't started" to "designs work AI-natively and trains others." The assessment places you on it. The program moves you up it.

Level What it looks like
1 · Curious Knows AI matters. Hasn't used it consistently. Interested but not activated.
2 · Tourist Uses AI occasionally, like a smarter Google. Starts from scratch every time.
3 · Regular Gets real value several times a week, but results are inconsistent.
4 · Builder Has set up a real workflow. Uses AI for strategy and decisions, not just writing.
5 · Fluent Reliable output on complex tasks. Becomes the "AI person" on their team.
6 · Native Designs work AI-natively from scratch. Trains others. Shapes how their field uses AI.

Most clients come in at Level 2–3 and reach Level 4–5. That's a genuine, measurable win — and Level 6 gives the journey an open ceiling.

The Key Insight

Getting ahead is a session.
Staying ahead is a practice.

Spanish doesn't change much — you can become fluent and coast. AI is a living language. New vocabulary (tools), new grammar (capabilities), new dialects (use cases) appearing constantly.

Fluency here requires ongoing practice AND ongoing learning as the language itself evolves. That's the honest argument for the subscription — and the honest answer to "do I need this forever?" You need it until staying current is a habit you've built. At Level 5, most people have it.
